Canchek FAQ

Q: What databases does Canchek search?
A: Our service includes these eleven databases:
Type of Data Source / Managed By
Canadian Sanctions Canchek Corp.
Cease Trade Orders (CTOs) Canadian Securities Administrators (CSA)
Canadian Insiders System for Electronic Disclosure of Insiders (SEDI)
Disciplined List Canadian Securities Administrators (CSA)
Domestic Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs) Canchek Corp.
European Union (EU) Watch List European Union
Global Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs) Navaera Corp.
National Registration Database (NRD) Canadian Securities Administrators (CSA)
Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC) US Department of the Treasury
Adverse Media Nomino Data Corp.
United Kingdom (UK) Watch List British Government

Q: Are all eleven databases available to all users?
A: The Global PEP and Adverse Media databases requires a separate Canchek subscription. The other nine are available to everybody.

Q: Where is Canchek's data hosted?
A: Our servers are located at a hosting company in Toronto, Canada.

Q: What are Canchek access levels?
A: There are two access levels. A 'manager' access level can access everything; a 'clerk' access level has access to a limited set of functionality.

Q: How do I setup new users, change access levels etc.?
A: Please contact customersupport@canchek.com for assistance.
